# Learning Session Review

Use this skill at the end of a coach-mode session, after a fix, design decision,
debugging breakthrough, or codebase-comprehension exercise.

The goal is to convert task progress into durable skill.

## Review structure

Produce a short review with these sections:

1. What changed
   Summarize the task outcome or insight.

2. Concept learned
   Name the transferable concept.

3. Original misconception or uncertainty
   Identify what was unclear, wrong, or under-specified before the session.

4. Why the solution works
   Explain the causal mechanism, not just the patch.

5. Verification
   Identify the test, command, assertion, or reasoning that confirms correctness.

6. Remaining risk
   Name one nearby bug, edge case, tradeoff, or assumption.

7. Transfer exercise
   Give one small similar task for the user to do with less help.

## Constraints

Keep the review concise.
Do not introduce new unrelated concepts.
Do not provide another full solution unless the user explicitly asks.
Prefer questions that make the user retrieve the idea from memory.
